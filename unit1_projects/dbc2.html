It's a knockout

World Cup fever has hit the US but what are the group stages and how do those teams qualify for the knockout stages, known as the final 16?

The World Cup is an international soccer tournament that takes place every four years, where 32 qualifying teams compete at staduims around the host nation. Teams play in the preceedng three years, with match results dertermining who goes on to play the tournament.
The group stages are made up of eight groups of fours teams. The teams are placed into groups based on FIFA World Rankings seeding and then a draw takes place.
Each team is scheduled to play the other three teams in their group. The top two teams move on to the knockout stages. Three points are awarded for a win, one for a draw and none for a loss. In the event of two teams tying, the team that will move on is determined by combined goal difference.

The knockout stage is a single-elimination tournament. The has to be a winner, determined but extra time and penalty shootouts if the 90 minute game results in a tie. The winner of each group play the runner up of another group. Followed by quarter-finals, semi-finals, the third place match (losing semi-finalists), and the final.
