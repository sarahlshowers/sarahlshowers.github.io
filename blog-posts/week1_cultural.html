Unit 1, Week 1 - Cultural Blog

06.28.14

It was the best of times, it was the worst of times...

DBC both excites me and stikes horror into the very core of my being. I find joy in the challenge of learning something new, the a-ha's provide such an overwhelming feeling of satisfaction but on the flip side - the dread, the frustration, the uncontrollable tears scare the living crap out of me.

There are others, amongst our ranks that have walked away from sucessful careers to persue this. I for one left 12 years in hospitality because I was tired and fed-up. I didn't have enough work/life balance to even begin to think about investing time in learning to program. I tried for six months and let me tell you, after 12 hours of being screamed at over chicken and rice there isn't much energy left to go home and lift a glass of wine to your mouth, let alone challenge your mind with learning to code!

I fully expect to be taking the first train to San Francisco and the last train home, seven days a week for the duration of the program, with additional trips to meetup before Phase 1 - minimum! I loved Shereef's analogy "If you're putting the cereal bowl back in the fridge after you're done eating it, it's time to go home!", that's pretty much what I expect, comeplete exhaustion from brain cramming. I'm new, so new and know that time is what I have to put in, in order to suceeed. I think to expect anything less of myself, or that kidding myself it will be easy is the fastest way I can set myself up to fail.

The mix of levels I see as a threat and as a huge advantage. I'm afraid I will fall behind but also see that those people can be one of the greatest assests to my learning but, only if I can get bridge that gap and use it to my advantage. I have taken alot from the early learning on growth/fixed mindset and Search Inside Youself and look forward to the teachings on the scope of empathatic learning

"You're smart enough but do you have the emotional inteligence... are you humble enough to say; I'm going to figure it out, I'm going to figure it out"
